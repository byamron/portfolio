# Languages ≠ Flags

## Duolingo redesign · Personal project, 2020

## The problem

Duolingo represents every language course with a flag. But which flag represents Spanish — Spain? Mexico? The 19 other countries? The mapping can't follow logic because no logic exists. And when it breaks down completely — the Arab League for Arabic, the state flag of Hawaii for Hawaiian — Duolingo reaches for flags most users couldn't identify anyway.

Visual: Grid of Duolingo's actual flag-language pairings, arranged to make the inconsistency undeniable at a glance.

## A known problem, an unknown priority

Users call this out regularly. Duolingo's CEO has acknowledged it's flawed. The W3C recommends against using flags for languages. Duolingo even hired a sensitivity consultant to redesign a character's body shape to avoid stereotypes — but this systemic issue remains untouched. It persists because it doesn't cause measurable pain and it's an industry-wide convention. Neither is a good reason at this scale.

Visual: Compact composite of Reddit screenshots, CEO acknowledgment, and Vikram redesign callout.

## Remove flags from course selection

Replace them with ISO-639 language codes — standardized, uniform length, immediately implementable. They drop into existing UI without changing anything else.

Visual: Before/after of the course selection screen — flags vs. ISO codes styled in Duolingo's typeface and colors.

## Add flags everywhere else

Don't remove flags from the platform — increase their use. Repurpose them in stories, roleplay scenarios, and loading screens to highlight the cultural diversity that the current system erases. Flags are great at representing countries. They're just bad at representing languages.

Visual: Mockups showing flags used well inside course content — stories, loading screens, roleplay scenarios.

## One change removes the problem. The other turns flags into an asset.

This is a personal project — but it doesn't have to be. The solution is low-cost, immediately implementable, and makes the platform better. Duolingo, the ball is in your court.
