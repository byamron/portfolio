Alert

<uw-alert>

Alerts are semantic messages that intentionally disrupt a user's workflow to notify users about statuses that require action or provide information relevant to a particular task or page section.

You have a past-due balance on your account.
Class meeting times overlap. Adjust your schedule to avoid conflicts.
Registration opens at 6:00AM.

Variants

Critical

Alerts with the critical variant are of the highest importance, and communicate that an item needs immediate attention or action from the user.

You have a past-due balance on your account.
HTML 

            

          

        


        
Warning

        
Alerts with the warning variant communicate extra caution before or during an in-progress activity, helping users avoid undesired results from the activity in progress.

        

          

            

              
              
Class meeting times overlap. Adjust your schedule to avoid conflicts.

              

            

          

          

            

              

                HTML
                
              

              

            

          

        


        
Info

        
Alerts with the info variant communicate useful information to help the user during certain tasks or situations without requiring immediate action.

        

          

            

              
              
Registration opens at 6:00AM.

Anatomy

        
Sizing

        
Alerts should occupy the full width of their parent container, usually a section or module on a page.

        
Text

        
Each alert contains a message which should communicate the following:

        
    
          
  • Why the alert is firing, in what context and what's affected
    • 
          
  • (Critical / Warning alerts) What the user must or should do to fix the problem, or relevant steps to resolve
    • 
          
  • Where to go for more information, if the alert's text is not self-explanatory, use relevant links
    • 
        

        
Color

        
Alerts convey a semantic meaning with a corresponding color: #511A1E (critical, deep red), #634E17 (warning, warm amber), and #0F2D62 (info, navy blue). Do not repurpose the alert colors for unrelated semantic meaning.

        
Call to Action

        
Whenever possible, alerts should provide inline access or relevant context needed to resolve them. Critical alerts should always contain a call to action. Warning and info alerts should provide one when the resolution is direct.

        
Dismissibility

        
Where practical and contextually appropriate, alerts should be dismissible and include an inline "x" on the right. In general, an info and warning alert should be dismissible. A critical alert should not be dismissible unless the issue has been resolved.

        
States

        
Alerts, when visible, only have one state. When an Alert has a call-to-action, the embedded action buttons should have a standard button pressed state.

      


      
      

        
Best Practices


        
When to use Alerts

        
Alerts are important, interruptive messages that draw attention and disrupt the user's workflow. They are persistent during active page loads, and will remain on the page until they are dismissed or the triggering condition is resolved.

        
    
          
  • Use for messages that interrupt the user.
    • 
          
  • For timely, important information or announcements; consider these characteristics.
    • 
        


        

          

            
✓ Do

            
Use Alerts for temporary, timely messages.

            

              

                
                
Registration Period 0 opens for adults and Degree/Graduate students at 6 AM.

              

            

          

          

            
× Don't

            
Don't use alerts to call out permanent content on the page.

            

              

                
                
A tuition hold has been placed on your account. Contact Student Fiscal Services.

              

            

          

        


        
Messaging

        
Alert messages should be succinct. Give enough context to understand the Alert and direct the user to the right place to resolve it.


        

          

            
✓ Do

            
Explain what caused the Alert and direct the user to the right place to resolve it.

            

              

                
                
Your account balance is past due. Registration is on hold.

                

              

            

          

          

            
× Don't

            
Don't use vague language or tell the reader things that are not actionable to the user.

            

              

                
                
Registration hold: please resolve the issue in order to register.

              

            

          

        


        

          

            
✓ Do

            
Add clear message controls and link to a reference page for more details.

            

              

                
                
Digital course materials have been charged to your account. Learn more.

                

              

            

          

          

            
× Don't

            
Don't use multiple, long sentences or mention multiple issues.

            

              

                
                
UW Bookstore Says that Digital Materials course charges for has a list of your courses enrolled and all of the digital materials. To manually access the required digital materials you can go through your husky card (or student account). Some courses enroll students in a trial group under the Inclusive Access program. Per University of Washington policy, UW Bookstore is charging the first 10th day after the 14th class day of the first day of classes.

              

            

          

        


        
Placement

        
Alerts should appear in the section or area in which they are relevant, directly under the appropriate section heading. If a page contains a global alert, it should appear at the top of the content area.

        

          

            
✓ Do

            
Place Alerts at the top of the section to which it's relevant.

            

              

                
Winter 2024 Schedule

                

                  

                    
                    
Course meeting times overlap.

                  

                  

                

              

            

          

          

            
× Don't

            
Don't place alerts buried in content where they might be missed.

            

              

                
Winter 2024 Schedule

                

                  

                  

                    
                    
Course meeting times overlap.

                  

                  

                

              

            

          

        

      


      
      

        
Assistive technology & keyboard functionality

        
Alerts include the role="alert" or role="status" attribute by default to screen readers and assistive technology. This ensures the content is announced immediately without requiring user interaction.

        
Alerts with call-to-action buttons should be completely navigable by keyboard alone. The Alert itself will not receive focus, but the interactive children must. The focus order is:

        
    
          
  • Hyperlink or call-to-action button
    • 
          
  • Dismiss button (if present)
    • 
        

        
The dismiss action is triggered with the Enter or Space key. The Alert component is configured to set focus to the top of the section in which it was placed if dismissed.
